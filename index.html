Krossover siQ (iOS)

While doing research on Krossover, I noticed how the company hadn't updated its siQ app for iOS in over 2.5 years. After playing around with the app, I decided to make a side project out of it. Below is the README of sIQ App Challenge. The purpose of sIQ is for an athlete to develop a better sense, aka sports IQ, by correctly predicting a play.

The first step of the challenge was scoping out what I thought the purpose of the app was. I also jotted down notes after testing the app out myself. This included bugs, UX and UI discrepancies and possible ideas for the next iteration of the app.


Next I typed everything out to make it more legible. These are some of my notes.

After some more research, I found an article on Sport Techie on the app and contacted the author. I discovered he actually worked at Krossover in Austin, Texas. He was kind enough to respond back to me.

My next step in the process was interviewing 5 candidates who I believed were the target user audience. They included past and current athletes, specifically football and basketball as well as other sports. The sIQ app focuses on football and basketball. These are some my notes.


I then organized and typed them up in a Word document.

After the interviews, I matched up repeat comments and from there, started mapping out a user flow. I drew 5 versions until I felt it matched what the app flow could look like.


After the user flows, I came up with some wireframes based off the responses.


These could next be used for A/B testing. The next step would be to create a prototype and have either the same or different candidates test out the new iteration of the app.


Another important aspect is knowing what the goal of the app is. This would come from meeting with the marketing and sales teams.


Gist Digital Accordion (iOS, Android, Web)

As a consultant for Gist Digital, the incubator of Accordion, I was assigned this project with a team of designers, as well as a marketer, with the task of developing a web application mirroring the existing mobile application, which is currently in beta. However after an extensive amount of user research and testing, we discovered that the target audience was amiss and went back to the drawing board. We wanted to make sure we had a clear picture as to who would be using this application and without forgetting about the MVP.
