Krossover siQ (iOS)

  • Date: June-July 2016
  • Client: Krossover
  • Date: June-July 2016
  • Service: UX/UI

While doing research on Krossover, I noticed how the company hadn't updated its siQ app for iOS in over 2.5 years. I wanted to add value somehow, so I initiated a side project on how to improve the UX and UI. Below is the README of sIQ App Challenge. The purpose of sIQ is for an athlete to develop a better sense, aka sports IQ, by correctly predicting a play.

The first step of the challenge was scoping out what I thought the purpose of the app was. I also jotted down notes after testing the app. This included bugs, UX and UI discrepancies and possible ideas for the next iteration.


Next I typed everything out to make it more legible. These are some of my notes.

After more research, I found an article on Sport Techie on sIQ and contacted the author. I discovered he actually worked at Krossover in Austin, Texas. He was kind enough to respond back to me.

My next step in the process was interviewing 5 candidates who I believed were the target user audience. They included past and current athletes, specifically football and basketball as well as other sports. sIQ focuses on football and basketball. These are some my notes.


Then I typed them up in a Word document.

After the interviews, I matched up repeat comments and from there, started mapping out a user flow. I drew 5 versions until I felt it matched what the app flow could look like.


After the user flows, I came up with wireframes based off the responses.


These could next be used for A/B testing. The next step would be to create a prototype and have either the same or different candidates test out the new iteration.


Another important aspect is knowing what the goal of the app is. This would come from meeting with the marketing and sales teams.


Gist Digital Accordion (iOS, Android, Web)

  • Client: Gist Digital
  • Date: June-Aug. 2016
  • Service: UX/UI

As a consultant for Gist Digital, the incubator of Accordion, I was assigned this project with a team of designers, as well as a marketer, with the task of developing a web application mirroring the existing mobile application, which is currently in beta. However after an extensive amount of user research and testing, we discovered that the target audience was amiss and went back to the drawing board. We wanted to make sure we had a clear picture as to who would be using this application and without forgetting about the MVP.


The first step in the process was conducting user research and testing. First, we targeted larger construction companies, but after multiple interviews, we discovered that this market needed too many additional features. We interviewed 3 segments — large commercial construction companies, smaller-based construction companies and DYI-ers. Below are some of the interview notes.


Based off the interviews, we created a spreadsheet to match recurring feedback. It was there we realized that the target user audience is smaller construction-based companies. Next, we created a features list spreadsheet, seen below, comparing the native mobile app to the additional features and how to integrate them in the web version.


From there, we updated the user flow for mobile and created one for the web.


In order to determine the best approach to re-design Accordion’s user experience and usability, we researched similar features in existing (successful) apps. These included: Trello, Slack Channels, Google Hangouts, Facebook Messenger, Whatsapp, Google Docs, F8 (Facebook Conference app), AirBnB and Periscope.


Next, we brainstormed and created wireframes for iOS, Android and the web. Originally, Accordion was only built for iOS. It's available for Android, but designed as an iOS experience. We decided it was best-practice to re-design the app for the Android-user as well as those users would be confused seeing the, for example, plus sign at the top right of the screen instead at the bottom right as a circle.

LeagueApps (web)

  • Date: Sept. 2016
  • Client: LeagueApps
  • Date: Sept. 2016
  • Service: UX/Content

While doing research on LeagueApps, I targeted the "Success Stories" page where I noticed a lot of inconsistencies both in UX and content. Below are some notes and how to fix and improve the site.


Every time a user visits the page, the stories load in a different order. Because of this, the user cannot scroll through all of the stories using the “Prev League, Next League” buttons as seen in the images below. If the user clicks on the first story, the arrow nav will say they have went through all of the stories after a few clicks.


What’s more frustrating for the user, is that “Prev League and Next League” buttons stop working as seen below.


The Read More links are not consistent. They should all be underneath the description text as seen in SixFifty Lacrosse, Iowa Elite Basketball and Big D Sports & Entertainment.

Some stories have periods at the end of the headline while others do not. There should not be periods at the end of headlines. “Professional” should be between single quotes — ‘Professional.’


The headline below does not have a period, which is correct, but the style is inconsistent with the above button. Not every word should be capitalized — and, a, to, should not be capitalized.


The intro text is in two different styles. The first paragraph should be in larger text. This is the case for multiple articles.


LeagueApps is spelled in various ways under the quotes section. In all three of these examples, none have it spelled correctly.

LeagueApps is spelled wrong in the footer (LeaguaApps API).


I presented my findings to the CEO, Co-Founder.
